Do you know how prepared you and your staff are to defend and present a clients
data in court?
Below are a series of questions that will help you determine if your firm is ready for
a case involving ESI. Keep inmind that these questions are only related to a firms
general readiness for cases involving electronic data. You'll still need to know what
to ask for from a client as well as the prosecuting party, which we can help you with.


1. Does your firm have inhouse support that knows how to preserve ESI evidence?
2. Does your firm have inhouse support that can forensically access ESI evidence?
3. Does your firm have inhouse support that data and drive formats?
4. Does your firm know what TAR (Technology Assisted Review) is?
5. Does your firm have a network to safely store ESI before litigation begins?
6. Does your firm log both user and file access that can be produced if needed?
7. Does your firm have a plan in place to quickly gather information from a client
    about where all of thier data resides?
8. Does your firm have a list of forensic companies or data specialists they can
    contact for assistance with ESI evidence?
9. Has your firm put together a budget in the event such companies need to be hired
    for a case?
